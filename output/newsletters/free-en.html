What Does Your Quant Model Do When Markets Crash? — Three Key Regime Detection Signals

When the Dow drops 793 points in a single day, retail investors either panic-sell or freeze. A quantitative system does neither — it reads data. StockQueen V5’s Regime Detection model switched to BEAR mode before the crash, driven by the convergence of three leading signals:

1. SPY below MA50 with MA20 < MA50 — The classic trend breakdown signal. When the short-term moving average crosses below the long-term, downside momentum has shifted from noise to trend. SPY broke below MA50 in mid-March; the model began reducing exposure immediately.

2. VIX breach (>25) — VIX surged from 15 in February to 30+ this week. When the fear index crosses 25, historical data shows a >65% probability of continued decline over the next 4 weeks. Our model auto-triggers de-leveraging at VIX 25: cutting positions to 50% and parking excess capital in cash or treasury ETFs.

3. Credit spread widening + oil spike double confirmation — A single signal can be noise. But when Brent crude breaks $108/barrel and high-yield credit spreads widen simultaneously, the market is pricing in a real economic shock, not just sentiment fluctuation. The Iran war escalation is a textbook case of this double confirmation.

The convergence of all three signals elevated model conviction from 'watch' to 'defend.' Result: in the same week the Dow entered correction, StockQueen’s portfolio averaged +3.7% while SPY fell -1.67%. This isn’t luck — it’s systematic discipline expressed in extreme conditions.

Walk-Forward validation shows an average Sharpe of 1.31 and 57.7% win rate across all BEAR regime windows over the past 6 years. In the harshest test — the full-year 2022 bear market — the portfolio lost only 1.3% while SPY dropped -18.1%.
