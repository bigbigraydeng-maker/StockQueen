V5: Expanding to 500 Stocks — Stress-Tested Across Every Market Regime

March 17, 2026 • 12 min read • V5 Upgrade Stress Test 500 Tickers • 中文版

StockQueen V5 marks the biggest upgrade since we started: the stock universe has expanded from 220 to 500 tickers, and every parameter combination has been stress-tested across bear markets, recoveries, bull runs, and late-cycle regimes. The result is a strategy that outperforms SPY and QQQ in every single market environment we tested.

The 500-Ticker Universe

A wider selection pool gives the momentum algorithm more opportunities to find the strongest trends. V5 more than doubles the universe from 220 to 500 carefully curated tickers, spanning ETFs, large-caps, and mid-caps.

The mix is intentional. Offensive ETFs (sector and thematic) give exposure to trending sectors. Defensive ETFs (bonds, gold, low-volatility) provide a landing pad when the algorithm rotates out of risk. Large-caps anchor liquidity and stability, while mid-caps supply the higher-beta momentum opportunities. Inverse ETFs allow the strategy to profit from downtrends without shorting individual stocks.

Walk-Forward Validation at Scale

With a larger universe comes more complexity. We ran a comprehensive Walk-Forward validation covering 40 months of out-of-sample data, testing 25 parameter combinations with 3-month rolling training windows. Two approaches were evaluated: an adaptive method that re-optimizes each window, and a fixed-parameter approach using the best single configuration.

The standout result is Bear 2022. While SPY fell 17.5% and QQQ cratered 29.6%, the strategy delivered a positive 12.5% return with a maximum drawdown of only 9.4%. This is the acid test for any momentum strategy: can it rotate into defensive positions fast enough when the market turns? V5 passed.

The Late Cycle 2025–26 regime produced the highest Sharpe of 3.44, suggesting the expanded universe provides more alpha opportunities in choppy, rotational markets where narrow leadership keeps shifting.

Why 500, Not 5,000?

It would be tempting to throw every stock in the Russell 3000 into the universe. More data, more opportunities, right? In practice, expanding blindly introduces noise, illiquid names, and tickers with too little history to produce meaningful signals.

Our 500 tickers are a curated pool. Every name meets minimum liquidity thresholds and has sufficient price history for the momentum lookback windows. The composition is balanced across offensive ETFs, defensive ETFs, large-caps, mid-caps, and inverse ETFs to give the algorithm enough variety to adapt to any regime without drowning in garbage signals.

Known Limitations

Transparency matters more than marketing. Here are the limitations we acknowledge openly:

Backtest ≠ Live Trading

All results presented here are from backtests. Real-world execution faces challenges that no backtest can fully simulate: partial fills, changing liquidity conditions, data feed delays, and broker-specific constraints. Treat these numbers as indicative, not guaranteed.

Past Performance Does Not Guarantee Future Results

Market regimes evolve. A strategy that thrived in 2022–2026 may face entirely new challenges in the future. Structural changes in market microstructure, regulation, or participation could erode the momentum factor.

Survivorship Bias

The 500 tickers are a curated pool of names that exist today. Companies that were delisted, acquired, or went bankrupt during the backtest period are not included. This likely inflates historical returns to some degree.

Concentration Risk

A 6-stock portfolio is highly concentrated. A single earnings miss or overnight gap can produce significant single-day losses. This strategy is designed for investors who understand and accept concentrated position risk.
