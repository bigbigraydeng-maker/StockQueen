Understanding Momentum Investing

March 14, 2026 • 12 min read • Factor Investing Strategy

Momentum investing is one of the most powerful and persistent anomalies in financial markets. Despite being discovered decades ago, the momentum factor continues to deliver superior risk-adjusted returns across global markets and asset classes.

What is Momentum Investing?

Momentum investing is based on a simple yet powerful premise: stocks that have performed well in the recent past tend to continue performing well in the near future. This phenomenon, first documented by Jegadeesh and Titman in 1993, has been observed across multiple decades, markets, and asset classes.

Why Does Momentum Work?

Several behavioral and structural factors contribute to the momentum effect:

1. Underreaction

Investors tend to underreact to new information, causing prices to adjust gradually rather than instantaneously. This slow diffusion of information creates momentum.

2. Herding Behavior

As more investors notice a stock's performance, they jump on the bandwagon, creating self-reinforcing price trends.

3. Anchoring Bias

Investors anchor to past prices and are slow to update their beliefs, causing trends to persist longer than they should.

4. Fund Flows

Institutional investors chasing performance create sustained buying pressure on winning stocks.

⚠️ Momentum Crashes

Momentum strategies are prone to severe drawdowns during market reversals. The most famous example occurred in 2009, when the momentum factor lost over 70% in just a few months as the market rebounded from the financial crisis. Risk management is crucial when implementing momentum strategies.
