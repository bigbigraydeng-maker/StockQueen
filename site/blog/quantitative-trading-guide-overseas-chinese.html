What is Quantitative Trading?

Quantitative trading, also known as algorithmic or systematic trading, uses mathematical models and computer algorithms to make investment decisions. Unlike traditional fundamental analysis that relies on human judgment, quantitative strategies process vast amounts of data to identify patterns and execute trades.

For overseas Chinese investors, quantitative trading offers several unique advantages:

  • ✓ 24/7 Market Monitoring: Algorithms never sleep, capturing opportunities across time zones
  • ✓ Emotion-Free Decisions: Eliminates psychological biases that often hurt individual investors
  • ✓ Data-Driven Precision: Analyzes 50+ indicators simultaneously for optimal entry/exit points
  • ✓ Risk Management: Built-in position sizing and stop-loss mechanisms

Key Quantitative Strategies Explained

1. Momentum Strategy

Momentum investing capitalizes on the tendency of stocks that have performed well to continue performing well. Our StockQueen algorithm identifies stocks with strong price momentum, earnings momentum, and relative strength compared to the broader market.

"The momentum factor has generated an average annual premium of 8.3% over the past 100 years, making it one of the most robust investment factors."

2. Market Regime Detection

Different market environments (bull, bear, sideways) require different strategies. Advanced quantitative models detect regime changes in real-time, adjusting portfolio allocations between aggressive growth stocks and defensive positions.

3. Factor-Based Investing

Beyond momentum, our strategy incorporates multiple factors including value, quality, and low volatility. This multi-factor approach reduces risk while maintaining strong return potential.

Tax Considerations for Overseas Chinese

Investing in US markets as an overseas Chinese investor involves important tax considerations:

  • ⚠ Withholding Tax: US dividends are subject to 30% withholding tax (may be reduced by tax treaties)
  • ⚠ Capital Gains: Generally not taxed for non-resident aliens (check your specific situation)
  • ⚠ FBAR/FATCA: US citizens and green card holders must report foreign accounts

Consult with a qualified tax professional familiar with both US and your country of residence tax laws.
