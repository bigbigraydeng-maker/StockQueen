2. StockQueen V5's Six-Layer Defense System

Most investors scramble during a crash. StockQueen's defense is discipline written in code. When the regime detection system identifies a bear signal, six defense layers activate simultaneously and automatically — no human intervention required.

Layer 1: Regime-Gated Allocation — 50% Cash in Bear Markets

The system classifies markets into four regimes based on SPY vs. MA50 scoring: strong_bull / bull / choppy / bear. When the regime flips to bear, the allocation matrix automatically shifts 50% of capital to cash. Mean reversion and event-driven strategies are completely disabled.

Bear Regime Allocation:

Momentum (V4): 50% | Mean Reversion: 0% | Event-Driven: 0% | Cash: 50%

Layer 2: Inverse ETF Hedge Overlay — 30% Short Exposure

V5's unique Hedge Overlay automatically allocates 30% to inverse ETFs (e.g., SH, PSQ) during bear regimes. These hedge positions operate independently from the TOP_N=3 stock picks — acting as a standalone insurance layer. In choppy markets, 10% is allocated; in bull markets, zero.

Hedge allocation by regime:

strong_bull: 0% | bull: 0% | choppy: 10% | bear: 30%

Layer 3: ATR Stop-Loss Tightening — Quick In, Quick Out

Individual stock stop-loss and take-profit multipliers adjust dynamically with the regime. In bear markets, stop-loss tightens from 1.5x ATR to 1.0x ATR, and take-profit shrinks from 3.0x to 2.0x ATR — minimizing loss exposure on each position.

Regime Stop-Loss Take-Profit Strategy
Strong Bull 1.5x ATR 4.0x ATR Let profits run
Bull 1.5x ATR 3.0x ATR Standard
Choppy 1.2x ATR 2.5x ATR Tighten
Bear 1.0x ATR 2.0x ATR Quick in/out

Layer 4: VIX-Based Deleveraging — Higher Fear, Lighter Positions

On top of regime-based allocation, a global VIX leverage adjuster scales down all positions. When VIX exceeds 25, all positions are reduced to 85%; above 35, they compress to 70%. This means even on top of the 50% cash from bear regime, extreme panic further reduces exposure.

VIX deleveraging thresholds:

VIX ≤ 25: 100% normal | VIX > 25: 85% | VIX > 35: 70%

Layer 5: Minimum Score Threshold — Only the Strongest Signals

V5's dynamic regime gating raises the minimum entry score from 0.1 (bull) to 0.5 (bear). In a declining market, only stocks with exceptional multi-factor scores make it into the portfolio — the system would rather hold cash than buy weak signals. This mechanism alone improved average Sharpe from 1.89 to 2.31 (+22%) in testing.

Layer 6: Trailing Stop — Lock In What You've Earned

The trailing stop activates once a position gains more than 0.5x ATR, then tracks the highest price at a distance of 1.5x ATR. If the market briefly rises then reverses, accumulated profits are automatically locked in — the system exits before giving back gains.

Combined Effect (Bear Regime + VIX > 25):

  • Actual equity exposure = 50% (regime cash) x 85% (VIX deleverage) = only 42.5%
  • Hedge position = 30% inverse ETFs
  • Effective net long exposure = 42.5% - 30% = just 12.5%
  • And only holding stocks scoring above 0.5, with stops tightened to 1.0x ATR

Compare to bull market: 100% net long, 1.5x ATR stops, 0.1 minimum score. Defense mode reduces risk exposure to less than one-eighth of bull mode.

4. Current Strategy State

Facing this week's selloff, here's how StockQueen V5's defense layers are positioned:

Regime Confirmation

The system requires 3 consecutive days of confirmation before officially switching regimes, preventing false signals from single-day noise. Once bear is confirmed, 50% cash + 30% hedge activates automatically.

Mean Reversion (MR)

MR only activates in bull regime. In current conditions, it's automatically disabled — no bottom-fishing in a falling market. 2025 WF data confirmed MR underperforms in high-volatility regimes (-3.6%).

Position Limits

Maximum 3 stocks held simultaneously (TOP_N=3), max 2 per sector. Even if one position crashes, single-event impact on the portfolio is strictly bounded.

Liquidity Filter

20-day avg volume > 500K shares, market cap > $500M. In panic selloffs, low-liquidity stocks are prone to flash crashes — we exclude them from the start.

5. What Investors Can Learn

1. Defense must be pre-deployed, not reactive

By the time the Dow has dropped 793 points and it's on the news, it's too late to hedge. StockQueen's defense activates when regime signals appear — 24-48 hours before the headlines.

2. Cash is the best hedge

Holding 50% cash in a bear regime sounds conservative, but 2022 proved the point: SPY fell 18%, our portfolio lost just 1.3%. "Losing less" is itself massive alpha.

3. Systems beat intuition

When CNN Fear hits "extreme fear," most people either panic sell or gamble on a bottom. A quantitative system is immune to emotions — it executes predefined rules with zero hesitation.

4. Multi-layer defense beats single stop-loss

A single stop-loss can fail in extreme conditions (gap downs). StockQueen uses six overlapping layers — even if one is breached, the others continue protecting your capital.

Historical Context: Over the past decade, following 28 similar bearish breakdowns in the S&P 500, the index gained an average of 16% over the next year. Crashes are never the end — but only if you survive the drawdown. That's exactly what a defense system is built for.
