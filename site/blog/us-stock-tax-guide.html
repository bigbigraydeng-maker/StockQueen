Understanding Dividend Withholding Tax

One of the most significant tax considerations for overseas investors is the dividend withholding tax. The US generally withholds 30% of dividends paid to non-resident aliens, but tax treaties can reduce this rate.

Tax Optimization Strategies

1. Utilize Tax Treaties

File Form W-8BEN to claim reduced withholding rates under tax treaties. For Chinese nationals, this reduces dividend withholding from 30% to 10%.

2. Prioritize Growth Stocks

Since capital gains are tax-free for non-residents, focus on growth stocks with low dividend yields to minimize withholding tax.

3. Tax-Loss Harvesting

If subject to US taxes, harvest losses to offset gains. Note: wash sale rules apply to resident aliens.

4. Timing of Residency

If transitioning to US residency, consider realizing gains before becoming a resident to avoid future capital gains tax.

Estate Tax Considerations

Non-resident aliens face significant estate tax exposure on US situs assets. Unlike US citizens who enjoy an $13.99M exemption (2025), non-residents only get $60,000.
